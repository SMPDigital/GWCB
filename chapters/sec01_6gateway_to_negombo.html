Chapter 6:

Gateway to Negombo

Negombo, with its large resident middle class and strong, independent civic spirit, is a town that has long prized education. After establishing a computer school in the town, it soon became clear that there was a large community of parents who favoured the idea of an international school for Negombo. Because of the town’s distance from Colombo, sending their children to a city international school meant either a long, tiring daily commute for the child, or a wholesale change of residence. Seizing the opportunity thus discovered, Gateway began searching for a tract of land suitable for founding a new branch. Before long, a location known as Kamkaru Sevana, near the picturesque Negombo Lagoon, was chosen, and construction began.

When Gateway College Negombo opened in 2007, it had only a single building housing six teachers and a modest thirty students ranged in classes from Grade 1 to 9; the Grade 8 class, that first year, had only one pupil. A-level classes began in 2008, skipping over the intermediate grades, which were added later. Expansion from this small beginning, however, was rapid, and staff had to be recruited, administrative procedures finalised and students registered with unusual urgency. Supervising this extraordinary effort was Principal Devika Alldis, who has been in charge of the school almost from its inception.

Attracted by the now well-founded reputation of the Gateway name, local residents flocked to register their children at the new school. By 2021, Gateway Negombo boasted nearly a hundred teachers and some nine hundred pupils; there were now three parallel classes per grade. Several new buildings had been added in the process. In 2019, Gateway Negombo became the first international school in Sri Lanka (and the only one in all of Gampaha District) to boast a 50-metre swimming pool.

The eco-friendly premises of Gateway College, Negombo.

Social responsibility & community values

Gateway quickly became part of Negombo’s famously active local community, involving itself in a wide range of appropriate social welfare activities. From rescuing and homing stray puppies to wetland and lagoon conservation, these projects are usually conceived and implemented by pupils, demonstrating their initiative and responsibility in true Gateway style. Teachers provide supervision.

Students and teachers alike also perform various acts of support to smaller schools in the vicinity, and children from these schools are encouraged to visit and take advantage of Gateway’s facilities and offered guidance and mentorship.

These activities are examples of the culture of community, responsibility and compassion that are so central to the Gateway ethos across its entire network. Over and above the basics of scholastic learning and personal development, Gateway College offers true values-based education, striving to produce socially responsible and considerate citizens of the world. These efforts have not gone unrecognised: at the national Edexcel awards, year after year, at least two Gateway schools are mentioned among the top five recipients. Schools in the Gateway network received Edexcel Gold Medals for community service in 2014, 2017, and 2019.
