Our Future,The Way We See It

CapZeus: Asteroid Capturer

PROPOSAL by Aditha Lathpandurage and Yash Wijayawardena
Year 8 (GCC)


INTRODUCTION

The asteroid belt is a ring-shaped region in our solar system, located between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. This region is occupied by millions of small solid objects and irregular bodies of different sizes. These bodies directly rotate around the sun and are known as asteroids or minor planets. It has been studied for many years and scientists have sent many mechanisms to explore the beauty and the hidden secrets between Mars and Jupiter’s orbit the “Asteroid belt”.

In the context of spaceflight, a satellite is an object that has been intentionally placed into orbit. These objects are called artificial satellites to distinguish them from natural satellites such as Earth’s Moon. Satellites are tasked to observe the solar system, planets and beyond.

In this project we will be discussing our design of a futuristic asteroid capturer that we see being used 25 years from now.

MISSION OBJECTIVES

The asteroid capturer CAPZEUS intends to capture an asteroid and send it to the ISS (International Space Station) for manual analysis. It is also built in a way to analyse complicated samples, samples of the asteroid the satellite intends to capture.

This asteroid capturer will play a vital role in supporting Earth’s limited energy by collecting rare minerals (e.g., gold, coal) and bringing it back to earth for further research and to stop Earth’s limited resources from running out.

SATELLITE DESCRIPTION

01 Solar Panels

These solar panels are made with silicon wafers. The most unique thing about this solar panel is that one solar cell can produce large amounts of energy to the satellite as the solar panels colour - gold - can take in more sunlight even in the darkest places in space.

02 Satellite Body

The satellite body is one of the most important structures of the satellite. What makes it unique is the body is designed in a streamlined structure so it can cut through the air in space and move faster and avoid Jupiter’s strong gravitational pull. Inside the structure are a nuclear reactor, laboratory, fuel tank and the other circuits with the battery. The body is made up of a mixture of aluminum, gold, and titanium. We have added aluminum because we want to make the satellite lightweight so it can travel faster and stop the radiation from damaging the body. We have put a layer of gold so the gold can reflect the radiation coming from anywhere, so it doesn’t harm the circuits inside the Satellite. We have also put titanium so it will be able to pass through the asteroid ring without being damaged - if asteroids hit the body of the satellite the titanium body is able to resist the damage and the satellite won’t be harmed.

03 Power

The satellite is powered with solar energy from the solar panels. The main source of fuel and energy is solar energy. Other than that, the satellite is steered and controlled by liquid hydrogen (LH2).

04 Telescope & Camera

The telescope is the main mechanism that absorbs the environment. The telescope can calculate the speed and coordinates of the location of the asteroid the satellite is intending to capture. The telescope is also the one in charge of scanning which asteroid is best to capture, and it also does thermal analysis so it can keep records of the asteroid.

05 Interior

The interior of the satellite has been divided to 6 parts: storage battery, fuel tank, nuclear reactor with engine, telescope compartment, circuits compartment, and laboratory. The walls that separate each mechanicals are made with a thick layer of aluminum.

06 Engine

The engine is the secondary power supply. As you know the engine is powered up by liquid hydrogen, (Refer Sub-topic 3 in Topic 3). But to make the satellite move faster we have added a nuclear reactor, which also acts like an engine. It is made with titanium and with a thin layer of gold and aluminum to ensure the radiation from the engine doesn’t go out of the nuclear reactor to the other parts of the satellite. There are engine exhausters which release the force provided from the nuclear reactor. It is made with aluminum to reduce the weight. There are thrusters at either side of the satellite to ensure balance to the satellite and to control it.

07 Expected Lifespan

This asteroid capturer satellite is expected to last for at least 50 years with the materials it is built with, and the technology and AI system it has.

08 Robotic Arm and Space Bag

The robotic arm is the most powerful mechanism in the satellite. It is unique as it can capture an asteroid with a 5-meter height and width. The robotic arm is made up with a fully titanium body to be able to resist the weight of the asteroid. The robotic arm is also equipped with 4 powerful motors, so the arm can bend in a right angle when capturing the asteroid. The robotic arm is equipped with a self-folding and un-folding bag made up with a thin foldable aluminum material so it can resist the push of the asteroid and convert it to potential energy to keep the asteroid in place in the bag. The asteroid capture bag is equipped with a closure door so it can store the asteroid safely. The closure door is made up with a mixture of thin aluminum and titanium, so the bag doesn’t damage itself when getting knocks from the asteroid inside the bag or from other asteroids from the outside.

09 Solar Grid

The solar grid is one of the most important structures of the solar panel as it protects the solar panels from getting damaged or scratched from asteroid pieces or chunks of asteroids. The solar grid is made of mostly titanium as it can resist the asteroids and there is a little bit of aluminum so it can be light.

10 Battery

The battery is the main source of energy and as the 4 huge solar panels provide large amounts of energy it should be able to store the electricity without wasting it. That’s why we included the battery. It is made up with a mixture of aluminum and gold. It can store energy with 2-years of battery lifetime if used wisely.

11 Laboratory

The laboratory is the place where the first mechanical analysis takes place before the manual analysis in the ISS. The laboratory is equipped with many things such as thermal analysis, microscopic scan, water scan and scanning for minerals. It is made with a mixture of gold and aluminium.

12 Cost Analysis

The predicted cost of this satellite is approximately USD 50 billion. The investment will be worth it as you get a big profit after your capture an asteroid.

WHY IS THIS FUTURISTIC?

This concept is very futuristic because: Still mechanicals have not captured asteroids and brought them to earth’s orbit or to the ISS (International Space Station) to do further analysis manually. That’s why this concept and design is unique as in another 25 years from now we hope this concept will capture asteroids and bring it to the ISS to do further analysis.

In the upcoming 25 years Earth’s resources like oil, minerals, coal, and diamonds will drastically decrease due to heavy use and overpopulation. So, this asteroid capturer will capture asteroids and bring it back to the Earth and restore our rare resources.
