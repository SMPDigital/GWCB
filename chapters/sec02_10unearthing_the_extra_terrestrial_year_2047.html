Our Future,The Way We See It

Unearthing the Extra-Terrestrial: Year 2047

WEBSITE by Thesath Mallikarachchi, Dillen Wijesooriya, Santhush Suraweera, and Asel Mallikarachchi
Years 8 & 11 (GCK)

That’s here. That’s home. That’s us. On it, everyone you ever heard of, every human being who ever lived. In the words of Carl Sagan, this tiny speck is the aggregate of all our joys and sufferings, thousands of confident religions, ideologies and economic doctrines. All the heroes and cowards, creators and destroyers, kings and peasants, children and teachers, inventors and explorers, dictators and corrupt politicians, rock stars, saints and sinners in the history of humanity, live here: on a mote of dust, suspended in a sunbeam.

Unfortunately, we haven’t managed to allow this precious information to sink in, because even today, in the 21st century with all its advancement, endless cruelties are inflicted by the inhabitants of one corner onto scarcely distinguishable inhabitants of some other corner of our dot. We act with immensely ignorant selfishness and the summation of such atrocities, personal posturings, our imagined self-importance, the delusion that we have some privileged position in the universe knows no bounds. Perhaps we will realise someday that all these ill-conceived notions are wonderfully challenged by the knowledge that life on Earth is merely a candle in the wind when faced with the enveloping darkness of Space and the quasi-infinite hands of Time. Our home seems so vast, and yet, we must come to the profound realisation that it is merely a microbe stranded in an incomprehensibly gargantuan Petri dish. Perhaps then, we might find ourselves united hand-in-hand by our common humanity, as minute differences slink away into the shadows of indifference. And, what’s more, it seems that we truly are a miracle, a statistical anomaly amid the barren lifelessness that plagues most of the cosmos. But the vital question remains, does life exist outside of Earth?

This project attempts to explore this vital question, venturing into the quasi-mystical realm of extra-terrestrial life and the ground-breaking implications of its future discovery while presenting our astrobiological theories to a 21st century audience using the latest research, interactive maps and 3D/AR modelling.
