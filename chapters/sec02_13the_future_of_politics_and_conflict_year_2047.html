Our Future,The Way We See It

The Future of Politics and Conflict: Year 2047

WEBSITE by Lahiru Senaratne, Thesath Mallikarachchi, Aaysha Fahim, Sineka Chithrasiri, Maleena Dassanayake, Hudha Rishard, Ayathma Adikari, and Ramzani Sariffodeen
Years 8, 10, 11 & 12 (GCK)


Successfully predicting the future of the extremely volatile international geopolitics and the constantly evolving and complex inter-state conflicts in 25 years is no easy task. But the MUN Club of GCK attempts to tackle this vast complexity at both regional and state-level based analysis, without any confounding generalisations, all presented in the form of an interactive world map. We believe that such predictions would be invaluable in this digital age, where we are rapidly making the transition toward becoming global citizens, ever more concerned with governance, that is, governance not merely confined to the national voting ballot, but on an exciting global scale. We hope that this project is one of many that attempts to lift the academic-literature based veil that (unfortunately) shrouds most highly technical subjects such as international relations and helps communicate advanced futuristic concepts in a more accessible format that is better suited to the audience of the 21st century.

EDITOR’S NOTE

In their discussion of the future of politics and conflict, these students of the MUN of GCK have focused on some of the world’s superpowers and political hotspots. They have created an interactive map which provides detailed explanations of their predictions on international relations. All the projections are based on what was happening in the world at the time the project was done as well as historical occurrences and patterns. The countries they discuss are coloured on the map, which can be accessed through the website. When you hover over any of the coloured areas with your cursor, a text box containing the predictions relating to that specific country will appear.
