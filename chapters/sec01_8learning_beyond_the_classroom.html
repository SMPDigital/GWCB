Chapter 8:

Learning beyond the Classroom

Griffy, the Gateway Griffin.

It is not only in class that a schoolchild learns. Gateway College offers a comprehensive range of extracurricular activities. In particular, pupils can compete in over a dozen sports, including football, basketball and swimming; every child is encouraged to take up at least one of these.

Gateway founded the Inter-International Schools Athletics Championship (ISAC) and continues to be a chief organiser and host of this annual contest. At this and other inter-school sporting fixtures, Gateway children have done exceptionally well, often making national headlines with their successes. Many have gone on to represent Sri Lanka in international competition.

Sporting morale was boosted in 2019 by the introduction of the College mascot, the Gateway Griffin (fondly referred to as ‘Griffy’). This chimeric but inspirational beast is an amalgam of the two animals featured on the College crest, the lion and the eagle. Embodying majesty and symbolising intelligence, power, honesty and discipline, the Gateway Griffin has been enthusiastically embraced by pupils and the staff community.

In addition to school sports, Gateway sustains more than thirty clubs and societies. Most are active all year round, holding programmes, exhibitions and competitions in every branch as well as across the branch network. Every Gateway school presents plays and concerts for the public annually, and the annual inter-house drama competition is keenly contested. Digital art and photography exhibitions also provide showcases for the talented.

Gateway has dominated the Model United Nations field, winning awards in both national and international forums. For the past five years, College students have been honoured with the highest number of awards presented. Gateway is also a regular participant at the All-Island Inter-School Shakespeare Drama Competition, often bagging awards and special mentions at this highly regarded annual event. Gateway pupils also bring credit to their school in debating contests held throughout the year. Students from all branches also participate in the annual Commonwealth Essay Competition and many have won prestigious awards.

Promoting the arts through extra-curricular activities.

Developing life skills

An important Gateway innovation of recent times is the ‘values, skills, attitudes and knowledge’ or VSAK programme. This eclectic educational initiative imparts valuable knowledge and life skills that are rarely part of Sri Lankan school curricula: personal time management, communication skills, sex education, teamwork and even cooking, to name a few. Each of these topics is explored through a mix of lectures, hands-on activities and games. The reception for VSAK has been very positive, so much so that it was decided in 2022 to implement a more structured VSAK programme in Years 7, 9, pre-A Level and 12.

Extra-curricular activities and programmes encourage young people to grow and mature in a fuller, more comprehensive way. At Gateway these activities are ascribed the same value as formal classroom lessons. A multidisciplinary approach of this type exemplifies the ‘Gateway to Learning’ concept and speaks directly to Ralph Alles’s vision of education that produces complete, well-rounded personalities.

In 2020, Gateway instituted a new programme, the Founder’s Award, open to A-level students only. This is a ‘launchpad’ programme that hones and tests participants in four important areas relevant to their immediate future after school: volunteering, internship, ‘skills and sport’, and adventure (the VISA cluster). The goal here is to prepare students better for the demands of tertiary education and adult life.

Taking pastoral care seriously

Beyond providing intellectual and physical education and promoting creativity, the true objects of education are the emotional and mental wellbeing of the child and definition of the type of adult they will grow up to be. Pastoral care is thus integral to responsible and responsive education.

The school auditorium gives students the opportunity to hone their artistic skills and shine.

It begins the moment a child joins any Gateway school, with personal observation and special provision to help them settle in. Staff work hard to connect with and establish rapport with new pupils. Ever observant, they are quick to note when something is amiss. A bond is established early, and the child feels comfortable approaching teachers and other authority figures when faced with any difficulty. Trained counsellors are also at hand to supplement the efforts of those who serve in loco parentis to pupils.

Setting the theme, year after year

In 2007, the school experimented with something novel – a theme for the year. Whereas the school motto is its perennial lodestar, the selected theme sought to express a general sentiment useful for guiding the day-to-day operations of the school, applying to all interactions and activities and motivating students and staff alike. Posters illustrating the theme appeared at strategic locations on the premises of all branches, and it featured likewise at school assemblies and other gatherings. Ever since 2010, the annual theme has been a regular feature and, at the beginning of each academic year, a new theme is decided on and published. Successful past examples include ‘Yes we can!’ (2007) ‘Do something for someone’ (2010), ‘Engage more, be more’ (2017) and ‘Brave the present, rise to the future’ in response to the challenges faced by Sri Lanka and the world in 2020.
