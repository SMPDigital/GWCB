A Work in Progress

Looking Back, Looking Forward

 

The twenty-fifth anniversary of Gateway College is an occasion worth celebrating: five schools in a quarter of a century is no mean achievement. Looking back, there is a sense of pride at how far the school has come and the many achievements the years have brought. Every year, Gateway students bag over half the awards at the Edexcel recognition ceremony for high achieving students. Gateway sports teams and athletes have won many medals and trophies at inter-school, national and international events. Graduates of the College have gained admission to prestigious universities across the globe. A notable number of alumni are high flyers in their chosen fields.

More satisfying than these accolades, however, is the impact the school has had on pupils and their families. Established at a time when international-school education was so prohibitively expensive that only the elite could afford it, Gateway College was one of the first such schools to levy affordable fees. This meant that children of many socio-economic groups gained access to an international-school education, enabling them to study in English and gain internationally-accepted certificates. Gateway opened a road to opportunity for these young people, allowing them to enter foreign universities and find lucrative careers.

As it marks this milestone, the College looks ahead to its next quarter-century. A new sports complex is being built on a nine-acre tract in Seeduwa. Work is under way on a 400m athletics track and a playing-field suitably equipped for cricket, football and rugby. In keeping with Gateway’s vision of holistic education, this project reflects the drive toward holism, the effort to encourage pupils to balance the academic and non-academic components of school life harmoniously.

In addition to helping children to grow and achieve their full potential, Gateway is also determined to take better care of the environment. The College is looking into making its buildings eco-friendlier and taking other measures to reduce its carbon footprint. Sustainability is high on the list of priorities for the near future; the aim is to achieve sustainability with and through the participation of all stakeholders.

But Gateway is not content with simply driving its own progress; it also takes seriously its role in shaping the growth and direction of international schools in Sri Lanka in general. To this end, Dr Harsha Alles has served as the founder-chairman of The International Schools of Sri Lanka (TISSL) since its formation in 2013. TISSL functions as the representative body of international schools in the country and provides a forum for collaboration across what is a very diverse segment.

The coronavirus pandemic revealed an important truth about education: buildings do not make up a school; rather, a school is made up of the people in it. As it embarks on the next twenty-five years, Gateway will continue to keep people at the heart of all their policies and endeavours. The members of the Gateway family remain at the core of every enterprise on which the College makes bold to venture.
