Our Future,The Way We See It

Our Views on Art & Entertainment: What Will the World be Like in 25 years?

ESSAY by Jasmine Balasuriya, Akithya de Costa, Rana Rillhan, and Siyara Rajakaruna. Year 5 (GCC)

Artistic expressions have existed for almost as long as humankind, like cave painting, shell engraving, and dancing around the bonfire with the idea of entertaining the dinner gathering. We can use art and entertainment as a form of expressing feelings and thoughts and as a way to spread important and powerful ideas among the community.

The art of the 21st century emerges from a vast variety of materials and means. These include the latest electronic technologies, such as digital imaging and the Internet.

IN THE NEXT 25 YEARS

A lot of individuals nowadays spend and invest their free time on social media. In the future reading and writing of messages will become a thing of the past. As a result, we will not be reading or writing. Instead, we will learn how to use computers in a creative manner. We’ll share our stories while sitting in front of a webcam.

Right now, we’re in the midst of a pandemic and more than anything, people crave meaningful connection. Their primary goal is to feel good. It’s inevitable that this will have an impact on entertainment. People may expect to see more entertainment and leisure activities tailored to avoid them feeling isolated.

It’s true that the estimated growth of bird watchers may be high in the next 25 years. Many species of birds are likely to go extinct due to habitat destruction and climate change. Spotting a rare species is typically the highlight of bird watching. Knowing that birds are on the verge of extinction in the next 25 years may make bird watching more exciting.

* * *

Another exciting example is the Star Wars- themed hotel that Disney will soon be opening. Upon check-in, guests will immediately become a part of an ongoing immersive theatre experience for the duration of their stay, with an experience that will be unique for each visitor. Temporary Forms is experimenting with machine intelligence in place of human directors, editors, cinematographers and more.

* * *

People express their thoughts in a number of ways. The question is how well people can communicate their thoughts. We foresee wearable devices that automatically draw or sculpt the ideas in your head in real time, eliminating the need for humans to create them with their hands. The expressions and concepts will be more precise as a result. Even people who are unable to draw can use this to express themselves in a more artistic manner.

* * *

At least once in our lives, we all want to go to Disneyland. We believe that a Disneyland or Universal Studios theme park will be built in Sri Lanka. This would increase the country’s revenue while also providing an excellent opportunity for Sri Lankans to spend their spare time.

How much do you want to see Michael Jackson do another live musical show? Or perhaps another Charlie Chaplin film? Engineers will be able to create life-size androids that resemble these departed artists in the future. People will be able to witness their favourite artists play live, even if they have passed away a long time ago.

We already seek comfort in art and entertainment in order to escape our stressful lives. People will be busier in the future and will have an increasing need for art and entertainment. They will also be more interested in classical music and art, and will pay more money to listen to it because it is more relaxing and pleasant.

* * *

We already have smart home devices at home that we can use to do certain activities. In the future, these devices will be developed into more intelligent gadgets that can sense our feelings and emotions. They will understand the vibe in a room and prepare the environment accordingly.

Imagine if all the robots in the world came to one place to show their talents. Yes, there will be a robot carnival just like the Rio de Janeiro Carnival in Brazil and the Robot Olympics in 2046. Both events will be robot-centric, while humans will be merely the audience.

* * *

We will be able to spend our vacations on the Moon or in space in the future. We may also be able to visit Mars if we are fortunate enough. Every country will have its own space station, and there will be hotels on the Moon and in space to accommodate this. There will be a unique package for viewing meteor showers from an outer space platform.

People will be able to travel back in time to attend their favorite concerts, stage plays, final matches of their favorite sports, Olympic events, and other events. We can witness a play by William Shakespeare in the Globe Theater during the Tudor period, see extinct animals like dinosaurs, mammoths, and dodo birds in prehistoric times, or go back in time to the time of King Dutugamunu using this technology.

Leisure activities like zero gravity games and fun activities will be introduced by 2046. There will be special amusement parks in outer space to launch these kinds of activities. Can you imagine yourself bungee jumping in outer space with your friends?

* * *

In the future, we will be able to replace stunt performers or body doubles with androids to lessen the risk element associated in making movies that feature stunt performers or body doubles.

We would probably demand that our entertainment be compatible with virtual reality. Our favourite animated cartoon characters have a big influence on how we conduct our lives through their speech patterns, clothes, personality traits, and so forth. We will be able to play with our favorite character using this technology. You may even invite them to a dance party. We’ll be able to converse with Queen Elsa, Tinker Bell, and Lucky Prescott like how we communicate now with Siri or Alexa.

* * *

In 2046, class teachers will teach art and entertainment lessons with assistant teacher robots for each student. For example, in addition to the human teacher, all pupils in a ballet or piano class will have an assistant teacher robot.

The above predictions are the dreams and hopes of four nine-year-old girls who want to live in a dynamic world where people value and embrace art and entertainment. With the rapid advancement of technology, the chance of having a cutting-edge art and entertainment sector is inevitable. People would appreciate these facilities since this is where they would turn to get away from their hectic and stressful lives.
