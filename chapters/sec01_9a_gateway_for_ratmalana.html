Chapter 9:

A Gateway for Ratmalana

In 2017, Gateway College Dehiwala still lacked playing-fields. The search for a suitable site expanded to take in neighbouring suburbs. At last a plot was found in Ratmalana, a twenty-minute drive from the school. Immediately after purchase, construction began on a football ground complete with pavilion and floodlights.

Some space was left over and, since Gateway was continuing to expand, it was decided to start a branch at Ratmalana. A new school building, a repurposed warehouse with an open plan designed for optimal ventilation and to be as eco-friendly as possible, was soon completed. Gateway College Ratmalana opened in October 2018, with classes from playgroup to Year 5. Grades were added progressively as the first pupil intake moved from class to class, so that by 2022 classes up to Year 8 were being offered. The school remains small, with two hundred students and 28 teachers. It is planned to keep adding classes up to IGCSE level (Year 11), after which pupils may move to Gateway College Dehiwala to complete their A-level studies.

Close links to Gateway College Dehiwala

Since Gateway College Ratmalana is a product of the quest to find playing-fields for Gateway College Dehiwala, the two schools have been linked from the outset. Ms Seneviratne, based at Dehiwala, functions as principal of both branches, with the deputy principal at Ratmalana in charge of day-to-day activities there.

Dehiwala students travel to Ratmalana for sports – as originally envisioned – while Ratmalana students frequently visit Dehiwala to take part in a range of non-academic activities such as swimming and basketball. Pupils also come together for annual events such as school concerts and annual Christmas carols.

The Gateway network

The opening of Gateway College Ratmalana brought the total number of Gateway Colleges to five. Although located in different parts of the country, they are closely integrated, forming a single, distributed entity. Management and staff work closely together so as to maintain consistency in administrative work, academic and extracurricular offerings, rules and standards, etc. Schemes of work are planned and designed collaboratively. Examination papers and marking are standardised. The school calendar is identical at every branch, with all schools conducting the same events. There are also many joint events and competitions, which the branches take turns to organise and host. Students and staff from different branches are in frequent contact and share many activities. The emphasis is always on belonging to the Gateway family.

Classroom arrangement promotes collaborative learning.
